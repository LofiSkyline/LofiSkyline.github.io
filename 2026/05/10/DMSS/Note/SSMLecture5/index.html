Lecture 5: Risk Treatment & Risk Appetite (风险处置与风险偏好)

Lecture 5: Risk Treatment & Risk Appetite (风险处置与风险偏好)

1. Risk Assessment vs. Risk Treatment (评估与处置的区别)

  • Risk Assessment (风险评估): The process of evaluating the set of risks (undesirable things), their likelihood, and their impact.
    • 中文解析：这是“诊断”阶段。我们要找出系统中可能发生的坏事（比如服务器被黑），并评估它发生的概率有多大（Likelihood），以及一旦发生会造成多大损失（Impact）。
  • Risk Treatment Plan (RTP / 风险处置计划): The process of choosing and applying a set of controls to address these risks.
    • 中文解析：这是“开处方”阶段。基于诊断结果，我们决定采取哪些控制措施（Controls）来应对这些风险。制定 RTP 的过程实际上就是做出业务决策的过程。

2. Four Strategies of Risk Treatment (风险处置的四种策略)

在面对风险时，安全专家可以采取以下四种动作：

Strategy (策略) Description & Key Definitions (描述与定义) 中文补充与案例
Reduce (降低) Controls that reduce the likelihood (probability) of a risk. e.g., Firewalls, encryption, staff vetting. 事前预防：降低风险发生的可能性。比如给大楼装防盗门（防火墙），或者对员工做背景调查。你尽力让坏事不发生。
Mitigate (减轻) Controls that reduce the impact (consequences) if a risk occurs. e.g., Backups, anonymisation. 事后补救：承认坏事肯定会发生，所以你要降低它发生后的损害。比如定期做数据备份（Backups）。黑客把数据删了？没关系，我恢复备份，这样损失（Impact）就变小了。
Transfer (转移) Making the consequences someone else’s responsibility. e.g., Insurance, outsourcing (PayPal). 甩锅/分担：让第三方来承担经济后果。比如买网络安全保险；或者你嫌自己处理信用卡数据太危险，直接外包给 PayPal（你付手续费，他们承担合规风险）。
Accept (接受) Choosing to live with the risk. An active strategy, not an admission of failure. 理性躺平：当防范风险的成本远远高于风险本身造成的损失时，企业会选择直接接受。注意，这是一项经过计算的主动战略 (Active Strategy)，而不是无可奈何的失败。

[!QUOTE]
“Mitigating risk is saying: yeah, bad stuff is going to happen. Let’s try to make it less bad.” (Transcript wk4/5)
(减轻风险的意思是：是的，糟糕的事情总会发生。我们只能试着让它没那么糟。)

3. The ALARP Principle (ALARP 原则：合理可行的底线)

ALARP: As Low As Reasonably Practicable (在合理可行的范围内尽可能低)。

  • Zero Risk is Unachievable (零风险是不存在的): You cannot ignore cost. Concepts come from health and safety (e.g., putting a “price on a life”).
    • 中文解析：追求“绝对的零风险”会让你破产。我们不可能为了防止员工在办公室摔倒，就把整个办公室铺上海绵。
  • Rationality (理性的平衡): Removing all risks is both expensive and difficult (e.g., North Sea drilling platforms).
    • 中文解析：消除风险既昂贵又困难。决策的核心在于寻找平衡点：增加的安全控制成本，必须小于它能降低的风险价值。

4. The Real Cost of Controls (安全控制的真实成本)

买个防火墙仅仅是硬件的钱吗？远不止如此。

4.1 Direct Costs (直接成本：明面上的账)

  • Capital (固定资产): Buying equipment. (买服务器、防火墙的钱)。
  • Revenue (运营支出): Maintaining and operating equipment. (每年的软件授权费、电费、运维人员工资)。
  • Training (培训费): Staff education. (教员工怎么用新安全系统的钱)。

4.2 Harder to Assess Costs (难以评估的隐性成本)

  • Opportunity Cost (机会成本): “What else could I do with the money?”
    • 中文解析：这笔钱如果不花在买防火墙上，是不是能拿去多招两个销售？或者做市场推广？这叫机会成本。
  • Side-Effects (副作用): Unintended consequences on the business. (安全措施对业务正常运转造成的意外阻碍)。

Direct vs. Indirect Side Effects (直接与间接副作用)

  1. Direct (直接副作用): Consequences of the control itself, regardless of user response.
    • 中文解析：比如公司实行了超严的邮件加密策略，导致出差的员工在高铁上打不开文件，错失了客户订单。这是策略本身导致的直接损失。
  2. Indirect (间接副作用): Consequences caused by Users Working Around (员工绕过) the control.
    • Crucial Point: “The workaround may be worse than the original risk.”
    • 中文解析：这是安全领域最可怕的事情。如果安全策略太反人类，员工就会想办法绕过 (Workarounds) 它。而他们绕过机制时带来的风险，往往比原始漏洞更致命。

5. Case Study: The CxO’s Mail (经典案例分析：高管的邮件)

The Scenario: A company imposes strict email encryption and VPN requirements. (公司强制所有人在外办公必须连 VPN 并用加密邮件)。

  • Problem: The CxO finds the VPN “a bit of a faff” (麻烦) and can’t check mail on his iPad while watching TV. (高管觉得连 VPN 太繁琐，影响了他在家看电视时顺便用 iPad 回邮件)。
  • Workaround: He asks his secretary to forward emails to his personal Gmail. (高管让秘书把他所有的工作邮件自动转发到个人的 Gmail 邮箱)。
  • Result: The data is now outside corporate governance, accessible to Google for data mining, and potentially illegal under regulations like GDPR or Sarbox. The security measure created a shadow IT risk.

6. Risk Appetite & Residual Risk (风险偏好与残余风险)

这是安全从业者最需要掌握的两个核心商业逻辑。

6.1 Definitions (定义)

  • Risk Appetite (风险偏好/风险胃口): The level of risk an enterprise is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives.
    • 中文解析：企业为了赚钱，愿意承担多大的风险？安全人员不应在真空中制定策略，必须对齐组织的商业目标。
  • Residual Risk (残余风险): The remaining likelihood and impact after controls are applied.
    • 中文解析：在你实施了所有安全策略后，系统里仍然剩下的那部分风险。

6.2 Who sets appetite? (谁说了算？)

  • High-Level Decision (高层决策): Risk appetite must be set by the Board or Senior Management, not the IT department.
  • The “Protection Racket” Trap (避免“收保护费”游戏): Security staff should avoid scaring management (e.g., “Buy this or we go bankrupt”) to get budget. This is known as a “protection racket.” Instead, provide options and quantify impacts.
    • 中文解析：别通过恐吓老板来获取预算。安全人员的任务是给老板提供清晰的商业选项：“老板，不修这个洞可能被罚500万，修它要花100万，您定。”

6.3 Residual Risk Statement (残余风险声明)

This is a formal document that lists risks not covered by controls.

  • Accountability (责任对齐): It forces the business side to either accept the risk or provide resources.
  • Avoid Cover-ups (避免隐瞒): The worst case for a CSO is not a security incident, but an incident senior management were never warned about.
    • 中文解析：这是你的“免责声明”，也是诚实的体现。明确告诉管理层哪些风险是我们没有处理的，将决策权和责任交还给业务方。

7. 🎲 Exercise: The Psychology of Risk (练习：风险心理学与期望值)

The Coin Toss Game (Slide 19 抛硬币游戏):

  • The Setup: I give you €1. You flip a coin $n$ times. If it comes up heads every single time, you must pay me €$2^n$. Otherwise, you keep the €1 and I get nothing.
  • The Math (期望值计算):
    • The probability of getting $n$ heads in a row is $(\frac{1}{2})^n$.
    • The payout if that happens is $2^n$.
    • Expected Value (EV / 期望值) = Probability $\times$ Payout = $(\frac{1}{2})^n \times 2^n = 1$.
  • The Lesson (核心教训):
    • Mathematically, the Expected Value is always €1, whether $n=1$ or $n=20$. It is a “fair bet”.
    • However, if $n=20$, the chance of losing is roughly 1 in a million, but the worst-case situation is losing over €1,000,000.
    • Risk Appetite: The expected value formula (Likelihood x Impact) breaks down for individuals and single businesses. You might accept a high-probability/low-impact risk (losing €2), but you will refuse a low-probability/catastrophic-impact risk (losing €1M) because the worst-case scenario exceeds your ability to survive.
    • 中文解析：这是对 Risk 公式 (Likelihood x Impact) 最有力的反驳！当 n=20 时，虽然期望值还是1块钱，但最坏的情况是你突然欠了100万欧元，直接破产。企业的风险偏好不是建立在统计学的平均值上的，而是建立在“最坏情况的生存能力 (Survivability)”上的。

8. Failure is an Option (失败也是一种选项)

In business security, unlike space flight, failure is often an acceptable cost.

8.1 Jeopardy Management (风险/博弈管理)

Smarter businesses decide when it’s cheaper to fail than to guarantee 100% success.

  • Example: A pizza shop promising “15 mins or free.” They’d rather give away the occasional free pizza than hire excess riders to cover peak demand.
  • 中文解析：公司有时宁愿承担偶尔赔偿的成本，也不愿投入无限资源。这叫理性的商业博弈。

8.2 Regulatory Reality (监管的现实)

Don’t be scared by theories of “4% of global turnover” fines.

  • Reasonable Precautions: Having an approved ISO 27001 Certification, a consistent Asset Register, and a valid RTP usually satisfies regulators (like the ICO) that you took “reasonable steps,” even if a breach occurs.
  • Goal: Be Careful (谨慎), not Perfect (完美).

9. Conclusions: Perverse Incentives (结论：反向激励的危害)

  • Total Cost is not just money: Never underestimate the ingenuity of users in foiling your intentions. (别低估了员工为了方便而破坏安全规则的聪明才智。)
  • Perverse Incentives (反向激励): Sometimes staff are driven by targets to do things bad for the enterprise.
  • Golden Rule (黄金法则): Measuring security staff only on security is a bad idea! Security must improve the overall operation, not just reduce theoretical risks.
    • 中文解析：如果安全主管的唯一考核指标是“公司不能被黑”，他最好的做法就是拔掉公司的网线。安全部门必须为业务运营服务，而不是以安全之名扼杀业务。
