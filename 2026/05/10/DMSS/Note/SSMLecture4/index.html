Lecture 4: Risks, Threats & Threat Actors (风险、威胁与威胁主体)

Lecture 4: Risks, Threats & Threat Actors (风险、威胁与威胁主体)

[!NOTE]
Terminology Consistency: The language in security is often confused. This lecture adopts the definitions from HMG IS1 (His Majesty’s Government Information Standard 1).

1. Core Definitions (核心定义)

Term (术语) Definition (定义) 中文解释
Threats (威胁) People and organizations who might do things. 指“人”或“组织”，即谁会发起攻击。
Compromise Methods (攻击手段) How they might do things. 攻击的具体方式（如贿赂、软件入侵）。
Risk (风险) The potential consequence to the defender of those things succeeding. 攻击成功后对防御者造成的潜在后果。

1.1 Threat Source vs. Threat Actor (威胁源与威胁主体)

  • Threat Source (威胁源): An entity that desires to breach security and will benefit from it (e.g., a foreign government or a criminal organization). 希望破坏安全并且将从中获利的实体
  • Threat Actor (威胁主体): The person who actually performs the attack (e.g., an agent of that government, or even an accidental insider).
    • Example: A criminal (source) persuades a system user (actor) to release information. The user is the actor, even if they were manipulated.

1.2 The Meaning of “Benefit” (利益的含义)

“Benefit” is not always financial. It can include:

  • “Feeling good about themselves” (心理满足感)
  • “Getting props” (在圈内获得声誉/炫耀)
  • “LoL” (纯粹为了好玩/恶作剧)
  • Non-Rational Actors: We must not assume all attackers are rational economic actors. Some ignore obvious risks for small rewards.

2. Risk Assessment & Calculation (风险评估与计算)

According to Professor Ian Batten, risk is the intersection of what could happen and how much it hurts.

2.1 The Core Formula (核心公式)

In the context of an ISMS, the basic logic for calculating risk is:

$$Risk = Likelihood \times Impact$$

  • Likelihood (可能性): The probability that a threat will exploit a vulnerability. This depends heavily on the attacker’s Capability and Motivation. (威胁利用漏洞导致成功的概率。)
  • Impact (影响): The consequence if the attack succeeds. This covers the loss of CIA (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability). (攻击成功后的后果，如经济损失、名誉受损等。)

2.2 Expected Loss & Prioritization (期望损失与优先级)

The result of this multiplication is often thought of as the Expected Loss (期望损失).

  • Ordering: By assigning values to likelihood and impact, managers can rank risks to decide which ones to fund first (Prioritization).
  • Example: A 1% annual chance of a $10,000 loss results in a risk value of $100/year.

2.3 Critical Limitations (教授强调的局限性)

The professor warns against “blindly” following the numeric result of this formula:

  1. High Impact / Low Likelihood (高影响/低概率):
    • Mathematically, a 1% chance of $10,000 loss equals a 0.01% chance of $1,000,000 loss ($100).
    • In Reality: Most businesses can absorb the $10,000 loss, but the $1,000,000 loss (a Catastrophic event) could bankrupt them. You cannot treat them as equal.
  2. Subjectivity & Uncertainty (主观性与不确定性):
    • Security is not physics. It is hard to assign an exact “0.01%” probability to a human attacker.
    • Most numbers in risk assessments are actually Guesswork.
    • Qualitative over Quantitative: Using a simple “Low, Medium, High” scale is often more honest and useful than a fake “six-point numeric scale.”
  3. The Insurance Problem (规模效应):
    • This formula works for insurance companies dealing with 10,000 houses (statistical averages).
    • It fails for a single business. You either have a fire or you don’t. You can’t have “0.01 of a fire.”

3. Threat Level Assessment (威胁等级评估)

The Threat Level (威胁等级) is a combination of two primary factors:

  1. Capability (能力): Can the attacker do this? (Technical skills, resources, tools).
  2. Motivation/Intent (动机/意图): Will the attacker do this? (Desire to harm, specific targets).

[!IMPORTANT]
Attackers need BOTH to be a danger.

  • Nation States: Massive capability, but limited intent against average individuals.
  • Disgruntled Employee: High intent, but limited capability (if exit processes are good).

2.1 Deterrence (威慑)

Threat actors are assessed on whether they are Deterrable (可威慑的).

  • Incentives for Deterrence: Threat of losing a job, security clearance, livelihood, or liberty.
  • The Prosecution Problem: Cybercrime is often not well prosecuted. Many see it as a “victimless crime” (如：年轻人觉得涂鸦火车是犯罪，但黑掉网站只是“不同”的行为).

3. HMG IS1 Threat Actor Categories (威胁主体分类)

[!TIP]
考试提示 (Exam Tip)：
你不需要死记硬背所有这些缩写（如 PI, PU, SSS）。HMG IS1 只是一个帮助你结构化思考的框架。
关键要求是：在面对一个给定的系统时（进行 Threat Modeling 时），你能区分出不同角色的风险差异。

3.1 Logical Grouping (逻辑分类方法)

为了方便记忆，我们可以将这 12 类角色分为四个逻辑维度（Logical Domains）：

维度 (Domain) 角色 (Roles) 核心风险特征
1. Physical Access
(物理访问类)		 PI (Physical Intruder), BY (Bystander), HAN (Handler), PWR (Person Within Range) 物理边界安全：他们能接触到设备、线缆或捕获电磁辐射。
2. Internal Users
(内部用户类)		 NU (Normal User), PU (Privileged User) 权限管理：他们有合法账号。PU 的风险在于“权限过大且难以约束”。
3. External Partners
(外部合作伙伴/消费者)		 IEP (Info Exchange Partner), SC (Service Consumer), SP (Service Provider) 信任边界：他们通过业务接口与系统交互。SP (如云服务商) 拥有对基础服务的控制权。
4. Indirect & Supply Chain
(间接与供应链)		 IC (Indirectly Connected), SSS (Shared Service Subscriber), SUP (Supplier) 隐形依赖：IC 包含所有互联网用户；SSS 涉及“邻居风险”；SUP 涉及“后门风险”。

3.2 Detailed Taxonomy (详细分类表)

HMG IS1 provides a specific taxonomy of people who could compromise a system:

Category (类别) Definition & Examples (定义与示例) 中文补充
Bystander (旁观者, BY) Authorized physical access but no business need to access the system. e.g., Cleaners, visitors, hotel staff. 拥有物理进入权限，但业务上不需要接触系统（如保洁）。
Handler (处理者, HAN) Business role requires physical access but not logical access. e.g., Couriers, hardware repair/disposal staff. 需要接触硬件（如维修、运输），但不需要登录系统。
Indirectly Connected (间接连接者, IC) No authorized connectivity but can use onward connections. e.g., Internet users. 无直接授权连接，但可通过互联网等间接途径发起攻击。
Information Exchange Partner (信息交换伙伴, IEP) Has a need to exchange info in support of business. Distinct from IC by “need” vs “ability”. 业务上确实需要进行数据交换的合作伙伴。
Normal User (普通用户, NU) Registered user with standard privileges in support of business objectives. 拥有标准权限的合法注册用户。
Privileged User (特权用户, PU) Manages applications, services, and defenses. Separate category because they are less constrained. 管理员用户。由于其权限极大且难以约束，被列为独立类别。
Service Provider (服务提供商, SP) Provides communications, hosting, or IDS. They control the service and could compromise CIA. 云服务商、ISP、托管商等。
Service Consumer (服务消费者, SC) Makes use of services provided by the FoI (Focus of Interest). e.g., website visitors. 服务的使用者（如网站访客）。
Shared Service Subscriber (共享服务订阅者, SSS) Authorized user of a shared service (like power/cloud) that the FoI also relies on. 共享服务订阅者（如云端的邻居租户）。
Supplier (供应商, SUP) Someone in the supply chain who provides/maintains software or hardware. 供应链上的供应商（可能在软件中留后门）。
Person Within Range (辐射范围内的人员, PWR) Within range of electronic/TEMPEST emanations or radio signals. 在电磁辐射范围内的人（涉及 TEMPEST 攻击）。
Physical Intruder (物理闯入者, PI) Gains unauthorized physical access (breaking in). 物理闯入者（暴力破门）。

4. 📝 Exercise: Threat Actor Analysis (练习：威胁主体分析)

Goal: Think about four different people or groups who might attack your business.

  1. Capability: What can they do, and how well? (Qualitative & Informal Quantitative).
  2. Intent: What might they want to do, and how likely?

💡 Reference Answer (参考答案)

以下是根据 HMG IS1 框架选取的四个典型角色分析：

Actor Group (HMG Category) Capability (能力分析) Intent (意图分析)
Nation State (State Proxy)
(Indirectly Connected - 间接连接者)		 Very High: Access to zero-day exploits, massive compute power, and professional teams. Strategic/High: Economic espionage (stealing R&D) or political leverage. Focus is on stealth and long-term access.
Disgruntled Admin
(Privileged User - 特权用户)		 Very High: Already has “keys to the kingdom.” Knows where the backups are and how to bypass defenses undetected. High (Personal): Revenge or financial desperation. Motivated to cause maximum damage or steal specific high-value datasets.
Organized Criminal Group
(Supplier - 供应商)		 Medium to High: Can compromise a small software library used by the company (Supply Chain Attack). High (Economic): Financial gain via Ransomware. They want to encrypt everything and extort money.
Amateur Script Kiddie
(Service Consumer - 服务消费者)		 Low: Uses automated “off-the-shelf” tools to scan for basic vulnerabilities (SQLi, XSS). Low to Medium (Ego): Defacing a website for notoriety or “LoL”. Random target, low persistence.

Key Takeaway (核心启示):

  • State Actors have the highest capability but selective intent. (国家级黑客能力最强，但目标非常明确。)
  • Privileged Users are the most dangerous “Internal Threat” because their capability is pre-authorized. (特权用户是最危险的内部威胁，因为他们的“攻击能力”是公司合法赋予的。)
  • Criminals focus on ROI (Return on Investment); they look for the easiest way to get the most money. (犯罪组织关注投资回报率，他们会寻找最简单的赚钱途径。)

5. Conclusions (结论)

  • Security is about people and groups of people: the WHO, not just the HOW.
  • Motivation is key: Motivated people will attack with whatever is at hand. Unmotivated people will not attack, no matter how easy the vulnerability is to exploit.
